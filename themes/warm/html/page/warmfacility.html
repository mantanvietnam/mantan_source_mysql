WARM facility

Context

Vietnam is one of the countries most severely impacted by climate change and natural disasters. An increase in average temperatures and subsequent sea level rise combined with population growth and rapid urbanization, has led to more frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, and an overall increase the risk of coastal and riverbank erosion, flooding, saline invasion and drought. In line with their international commitments on climate change and Sustainable Development Goals, the European Union and French Development Agency (AFD) are actively supporting Vietnam on climate change mitigation and adaptation

Description

The WARM Facility supports the preparation and implementation of strategic infrastructure investment projects through technical assistance and capacity building in the areas of water and natural resources management, urban resilience and green growth management in response to key local challenges such as flooding, urban inundation, coastal and riverbank erosion, and saline invasion, exacerbated by climate change impacts.

Furthermore, the WARM Facility supports policy dialgues at ministry level through technical exchanges, thematic workshops and implementation of action plans to improve legal framework on (i) integrated water resource management; (ii) integrated coastal zone management; and (iii) urban resilience and green growth with the technical ministries like MONRE, MARD and MOC) through:

  • (i) bottom-up approach: based on field implementation of the funded projects with good practices and lessons learnt to make recommendations to the relevant Ministries on technical and legal issues for the related law and policy improvements;

  • (ii) top-down approach: review of legal framework from the view of policy makers and technical ministries; and

  • (iii) peer-to-peer exchange of technical and management knowledge and experience from TeamEurope member states in the three related topics.

In this framework, the EU has contributed a 20 million euro grant which is supported by a 200 million euro loan from AFD to support the projects.

This facility is the first of its kind to be implemented by AFD in Vietnam. This innovative programmatic approach allows for greater efficiency and project quality, serving programming that is aligned with the priorities of the Vietnamese government and Team Europe, and AFD.

WARM Impacts

Economically, full advantage of the natural resource management is taken along with assurance of economic and financial profitability and sustainability of the investments. Socially, WARM helps to maximise the social impact of investment projects through the support of structural social improvements, such as gender equality or participative planning and management of the natural resources or infrastructures. The need of all users of the natural resources (water, land and/or forest), including marginalized and poorer user groups, is taken in full consideration in allocating the natural resources. This will contribute to prevent conflicts among natural resource users which are a major challenge in a context of climate change. Financially, WARM is critical in leveraging AFD funding for projects supporting the resilience of territories/cities.

Environmentally, WARM notably contributes to:

  • Improve the resilience of communities and their livelihoods in areas vulnerable to climate change
  • Prevent and control coastal zones erosion
  • Reinforce integrated management of water-resources
  • Foster resilient urban development projects and adaptation to climate change of cities
  • Prevent and control natural disasters in vulnerable provinces
  • Support policy dialogue and strategic dialogue related to water and natural resources management.

In short, the expected impact of the Facility is to improve the resilience of Vietnamese vulnerable areas to water-related climatic hazards, through faster and more efficient projects at local level and the integration of projects results into policies at national level.

Project objectives and content

The WARM Facility aims at improving the resilience of populations and economic activities to water-related hazards through more resilient cities and territories to climate change and natural hazards, improved and integrated water management and/or integrated coastal zone management.

The main objectives are:

- At local level, to improve the resilience of communities in areas vulnerable to climate change and to support and optimise environmental and social performance of projects related to water and natural resources management in a context of climate change. The WARM Facility will support Local Authorities (provinces, cities...) in the preparation (C1), implementation (C2) and financing (C3) of more efficient projects in the fields of integrated coastal zone management, integrated water resources management, and resilience of cities to climate change in a context of increasing risks related to climatic hazards and natural disasters.
- At central level, to support policy and strategic dialogue related to water and natural resources management through capitalisation and policy dialogue (C4).

The programmatic approach of the Facility will include 4 (four) main components:

Component 1

Projects preparation:

Support to enhance the quality of the preparatory studies for projects identified and ensure that Local Authorities and relevant Vietnamese partners (including local communities) maximize the environmental and social impacts of the projects prepared and secure financing for significant water and natural resources management projects;

Component 2

Projects Implementation support:

Support to projectsâ€™ implementation with technical assistance, ensure intellectual production, capacity building, training of trainers and other accompanying measures, to ensure the sustainability of the investments financed by AFD loans;

Component 3

Investments:

Through this component, separate AFD loans would finance the investments in infrastructure for several projects with local authorities in the fields of integrated coastal zone management, integrated water resources management and natural disasters and climatic hazards prevention in vulnerable territories (including cities). Several potential investment projects have already been pre- identified;

Component 4

Policy dialogue, crosscutting issues and capitalisation:

Support policy or strategic dialogue, improve the knowledge in the thematic sectors concerned by the Facility, and improve coordination and accountability.
